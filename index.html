Founding of the Arizona Air National Guard

Beginning with the American Revolution's "Minutemen" at Lexington and Concord, the citizen-soldier, the National Guard, has long been a critical resource of the American military.

With the advent of aviation early in the 20th century, individuals quickly began to see the application of the airplane as miltary weapon. Many of these early pioneers were National Guardsman.

By 1912, only three years after its organization, the First Signal Company, New York National Guard, got their first airplane off the ground. Units in California and Missouri were soon to follow. Renamed the First Aero Company, New York National Guard, the squadron was mobilized in 1915 to combat the border raids of Pancho Villa.

It was World War Two, though, that saw the full potential of the airplane's military use recognized. In 1941, 4800 men from 29 National Guard units helped begin what was to become the U.S. Army Air Force. Following the war, a number of WWII Army Air Force squadrons were reorganized into Air Guard units and established in several states around the country.

The 412th Fighter Squadron, a unit that had earned extensive combat flying honors in Europe was redesignated the 197th Fighter Squadron, and on December 12, 1946, became the first unit of the Arizona Air National Guard, with an authorized strength of 300 enlisted and 50 officers.

Today

161st ARW

The 161st Air Refueling Wing’s federal mission is to organize, train and equip units and individuals to meet worldwide requirements in support of the Air Force’s Global Reach mission. The KC-135 is the core air refueling platform for the United States Air Force and has excelled in this role for more than 50 years. The 161st operates the KC-135 “R” model, the latest version of the aircraft. Depending on the fuel storage configuration, the KC-135 can carry up to 200,000 pounds of JP-8 fuel, and 83,000 pounds of cargo, or personnel.

162nd W

The Morris Air National Guard Base is home to the Air National Guard's premier F-16 fighter pilot training unit, the 162nd Wing. It is one of the largest ANG wings in the country and resides next to the Tucson International Airport.

The 162nd is the “face of the USAF to the world”, providing the best-trained coalition war-fighting partners for the United States Air Force. The wing has trained pilots from 25 countries that fly the F-16 today while developing strategic partnerships and building strong international relationships based on performance, friendship, and trust.

214th ATKS

The 214th Attack Group employs the MQ-9 Reaper through remote split operations from Davis-Monthan Air Force Base, Ariz and from Ft. Huachuca in Sierra Vista, Ariz. The unit also flies the RC-26 Condor aircraft from Tucson International Airport The unit flies daily combat missions, providing troops on the ground with around the clock intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance and precision attack and local domestic Incident Assessment and Awareness flights over the United States in support of national and state objectives.

The unit’s launch and recovery facility out of Libby Army Airfield at Fort Huachuca operates the remotely piloted aircraft to train aircrew for mission currency and conduct takeoffs and landings, one of five such units in the United States. The unit is also prepared to support local agencies and fulfill state mission requirements.
